# Tesla Canada — Pre-computed cost scenarios (Model 3 / Model Y)

Snapshot **2026-06-21**, all CAD. Financing math validated to the dollar against tesla.com/en_ca (finplat). **Province = Ontario (13% HST)** for tax columns — change province shifts every tax figure (AB 5%, BC 12%, QC ~15%, Maritimes 15%).

> **What's a Tesla cost vs. any-car cost:** the *financing payment* and *tax* are Tesla's. *Insurance, charging, tires, plates* you'd pay on any car — shown so the all-in is honest, but strip them mentally when comparing Tesla-to-Tesla. Insurance/charging are **estimates** (vary by driver/province). See `tesla_other_costs.md` for every line item + sources.

## 3. Down-payment sensitivity

More down → lower monthly, but you've parked cash in a depreciating car. Totals barely move on finance (you pay roughly the same either way — down just shifts timing); on a lease, more down mostly buys a lower monthly + a bigger loss if the car is totalled early.

## 4. 8-year total cost of ownership — apples to apples (car only, incl. HST, default down)

Common 96-month horizon. **Finance**: pay it off in 8 yr, keep the car. **Lease**: two back-to-back 48-mo leases (always in a ~new car). **Cash**: pay once, keep. *Net* subtracts an estimated resale value at year 8 (~28% of MSRP for keep-paths; lease owns nothing). Resale % is a rough estimate.

| Vehicle | Finance (gross / net after resale) | Lease ×2 (gross) | Cash (gross / net) |
|---|--:|--:|--:|
| Model 3 Premium RWD | $56,995 / **$45,938** | $70,100 | $47,609 / **$36,552** |
| Model 3 Premium AWD | $71,432 / **$57,435** | $83,828 | $59,474 / **$45,477** |
| Model 3 Performance | $105,804 / **$84,807** | $125,166 | $87,724 / **$66,727** |
| Model Y RWD | $71,248 / **$57,251** | $82,708 | $59,474 / **$45,477** |
| Model Y Premium AWD | $91,871 / **$73,674** | $107,214 | $76,424 / **$58,227** |
| Model Y Performance | $105,620 / **$84,623** | $125,930 | $87,724 / **$66,727** |

**Takeaways:** Finance-and-keep is the cheapest way to actually *own* (lowest net once you bank the resale). Lease costs more over 8 yr but keeps you in a new car with warranty and lower monthly; its real edge is **flexibility + you only pay HST on what you use**, not the residual. Cash avoids all interest but ties up ~$48–88k that could earn elsewhere.
